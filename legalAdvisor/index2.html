Agreement

An official deal between two or more parties that explains what they have to do about a specific thing. These deals can be different, like contracts or partnership agreements, and they are usually legal and can be enforced

Arbitration Agreements

A legal contract where people agree to solve problems with a middle person (arbitrator) instead of going to court. The middle person makes decisions that both sides usually have to follow

Explore

Bonds

A legal promise to pay back borrowed money with extra (interest) on a certain date. Bonds can be from the government or companies, and people often trade them in financial markets. They have rules and might protect the people who own them

Explore

Dishonour of cheque

When a bank can't give the money promised in a written order (cheque). This happens when there's not enough money in the person's bank account or other reasons like a wrong signature, an old cheque, or a "stop" order. It's against the law in many places and can be serious

Explore

Criminal Court Forms

Papers used in criminal court cases to write down and deal with different parts of the case, like charges, what people say, evidence, and what the court says. These forms help keep track of things and make sure the law is followed

Explore

Gift Deeds

A paper that legally moves ownership of something valuable from one person to another without any money. This paper says that the giver wants to give, and the receiver wants to get. People often use it for planning their property and giving presents
